3.  **Is the claim boundary correct: accept the bridge as a compositional RTDL design step, but do not claim paper-speedup or broad RT-core DBSCAN acceleration because pure CuPy remains faster on these rows?**
    *   **Answer:** Yes, the claim boundary is correct. The `docs/reports/goal2401_rt_dbscan_optix_summary_bridge_pod_evidence_2026-05-19.md` explicitly states an `accept-with-boundary` verdict, detailing that it is not a paper reproduction, not a paper-speedup claim, and not a broad RT-core DBSCAN acceleration claim, as the pure CuPy device-grid baseline remains faster on the tested 4096-point synthetic datasets. This position is explicitly verified by `tests/goal2401_rt_dbscan_optix_summary_bridge_pod_evidence_test.py`.

4.  **Is the next gap correctly stated as device-resident OptiX output handoff or cheaper repeated prepared summary execution?**
    *   **Answer:** Yes, the next gap is correctly stated. The "Design Lesson" section in `docs/reports/goal2401_rt_dbscan_optix_summary_bridge_pod_evidence_2026-05-19.md` clearly identifies this: "for RT-DBSCAN-level performance, the OptiX summary output must become a device-resident handoff into the component continuation, or the prepared OptiX summary path must become much cheaper across repeated calls."

## Conclusion

The Goal2400/Goal2401 work successfully introduces a generic bridge that composes OptiX fixed-radius ranked summaries with a CuPy device-grid component continuation. This bridge effectively avoids the materialization of all neighbor rows, which was a significant architectural gap. While it represents a compositional design step and is validated for correctness, the current performance profile (where OptiX summary setup dominates and pure CuPy remains faster) correctly indicates that further optimization, specifically device-resident OptiX output handoff or cheaper repeated prepared summary execution, is necessary to achieve paper-level performance claims. The work is robustly tested and documented, with clear boundaries on what can and cannot be claimed.
