# Gemini Review: v3.0 Frechet Lab Lessons

**Reviewer Identity:** Gemini
**Independence:** This review is independent from Codex authoring.

**Verdict:** accept

**Review Comments:**

The planning note "v3.0 Planning Note: Frechet Lab Lessons After v1.8" provides a clear and well-reasoned analysis of the Frechet lab's outcomes and their implications for future development.

1.  **v2.0 Focus:** The note effectively maintains focus on the v2.0 roadmap. It repeatedly emphasizes v2.0 as the active roadmap and explicitly states that the v3.0 insights are exploratory and not commitments, clearly delineating the boundary between current priorities and future considerations.

2.  **App-Agnostic Primitives:** The proposed future primitives in the "Primitive Insights" section are thoughtfully named and framed with app-agnostic wording. This approach aligns with the goal of creating generic, reusable components rather than application-specific logic.

3.  **Avoiding Overclaiming:** The note is commendably cautious in its claims. It explicitly states that "this particular Frechet split is not a real speedup against optimized C++ on real GeoLife trajectories" and includes a strong "Non-Claims" section that directly advises against overstating acceleration capabilities on real datasets.

4.  **Refining v3.0 Extension Idea:** The "v3.0 Extension Insight" section successfully refines the concept of custom engine extensions beyond the simplistic "PCIe slot" metaphor. It introduces a more comprehensive model encompassing typed device payload contracts, backend-specific shader entry contracts, compact output, conformance tests, and cost-model/fallback stories, which reflects a mature understanding of extension complexities.

5.  **Public Roadmap Safety:** The "Non-Claims" section is robust and crucial for public roadmap safety. It clearly articulates what should and should not be communicated, preventing potential misinterpretations or premature expectations regarding RT-core acceleration, shader plug-ins, or the timing of v3.0. This contributes significantly to managing external messaging effectively.

Overall, the document is well-articulated, provides valuable insights from the lab, and responsibly frames its implications for the roadmap, particularly in its careful distinction between v2.0 and v3.0 and its emphasis on non-claims.
