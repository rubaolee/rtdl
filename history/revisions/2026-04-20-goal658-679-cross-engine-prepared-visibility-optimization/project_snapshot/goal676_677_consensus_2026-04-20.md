## Consensus Verdict

Codex, Claude, and Gemini agree that Goal676/677 is acceptable.

The closure report and public-doc refresh correctly describe the current-main
prepared/prepacked visibility/count optimization work for Apple RT, OptiX,
HIPRT, and Vulkan without crossing the honesty boundary.

## Accepted Claims

- Prepared build-side data, prepacked probe-side data, and reduced output
  contracts are now the demonstrated optimization direction for repeated
  visibility/count-style apps.
- Apple RT has a measured scalar blocked-ray count win on the tested Apple M4
  harness, but not a full emitted-row speedup claim.
- OptiX has a strong prepared/prepacked scalar-count result on Linux GTX 1070,
  but that host has no RT cores.
- HIPRT has a large prepared 2D any-hit repeated-query improvement on
  HIPRT/Orochi CUDA, but this is not AMD GPU validation.
- Vulkan has a measured prepared/prepacked improvement, but the tuple-ray
  prepared path alone is not claimed faster.

## Rejected Generalizations

- No broad speedup across all RTDL workloads.
- No DB or graph speedup from visibility/count evidence.
- No one-shot-call speedup claim from prepared repeated-query evidence.
- No full emitted-row Apple RT speedup from scalar count evidence.
- No native backend acceleration claim for `rt.reduce_rows(...)`.
